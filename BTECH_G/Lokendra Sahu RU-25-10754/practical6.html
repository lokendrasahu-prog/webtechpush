Natural Disaster

A natural disaster is a catastrophic event that occurs due to natural processes of the Earth.
These events can lead to loss of life, destruction of property, and significant economic impacts.
They are typically classified based on their causes, which can be weather-related, geological, or biological.

types of DISASTERS

  • Earthquakes: Sudden shaking of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates.
  • Floods: Overflow of water onto normally dry land, often due to heavy rainfall or melting snow.
  • Hurricanes/Typhoons: Powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds and heavy rain.
  • Tornadoes: Violently rotating columns of air that extend from thunderstorms to the ground.
  • Wildfires: Uncontrolled fires that spread rapidly through vegetation, often exacerbated by dry conditions.
  • Volcanic Eruptions: Explosive events that occur when there is a sudden release of magma from beneath the Earth's crust.
  • Droughts: Extended periods of deficient rainfall relative to the statistical multi-year average for a region.
